The Top 10 Interior Design Trends for 2024

March 15, 2024·6 min read

The world of interior design never stands still. As we move deeper into 2024, several exciting trends are reshaping how we think about our living and working spaces. From a renewed focus on sustainability to the warm embrace of tactile materials, here are the trends our design team is most excited about.

1. Warm Minimalism

Gone are the cold, stark whites of traditional minimalism. In its place, a warmer approach that pairs clean lines with rich textures, earthy tones, and natural materials. Think plaster walls in warm cream, linen curtains in oatmeal, and furniture in solid walnut — minimal in form but maximal in feeling.

2. Sustainable Luxury

High-end design no longer means environmental compromise. Clients increasingly demand materials that are both exquisite and responsibly sourced — reclaimed hardwoods, recycled metals, artisanally produced ceramics, and low-VOC finishes. Sustainability has become a marker of true luxury.

3. Curves & Organic Forms

Sharp angles are giving way to soft, sculptural shapes. Arched doorways, rounded furniture, undulating walls, and circular motifs create spaces that feel nurturing and organic. This trend reflects a broader desire for interiors that soothe rather than stimulate.

4. Biophilic Design

Bringing nature indoors continues to gain momentum. Beyond potted plants, biophilic design integrates natural light, water features, living walls, natural ventilation, and organic materials to create a genuine connection between inhabitants and the natural world.

5. Artisan Craftsmanship

Mass-produced uniformity is out. Handcrafted imperfection is in. From hand-poured concrete countertops to individually glazed tiles and bespoke joinery, the evidence of human touch adds depth, character, and story to modern interiors.

"Design is not about decoration — it's about creating environments that enhance the way we live, think, and feel." — Élise Beaumont, Creative Director

6. Earth Tones & Natural Palettes

Terracotta, olive, ochre, clay, sage — the colour palette of 2024 draws directly from the landscape. These grounding hues create interiors that feel restful and timeless, working beautifully with natural stone and timber.

7. Multi-Functional Spaces

Post-pandemic flexibility remains essential. Spaces that seamlessly transition from home office to living room, from dining area to creative studio. Clever storage, modular furniture, and thoughtful zoning make this possible without sacrificing aesthetics.

8–10. More Trends to Watch

Looking ahead, we're also seeing a resurgence of statement lighting as functional art, dark and moody palettes for intimate spaces, and smart home integration that's invisible rather than intrusive. Each of these trends shares a common thread: design that serves human wellbeing above all.

Élise Beaumont
Co-Founder & Creative Director at Lumière

With 18+ years in luxury design, Élise leads Lumière's creative vision and shapes trends across residential and hospitality design globally.
